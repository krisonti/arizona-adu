Why now

Arizona just made the backyard rentable.

A 2025 state law rewrote ADU rules across every major Arizona city. For a landlord who already owns a Phoenix lot, it removed the barriers that used to make a second unit slow, discretionary, or impossible.

01 · By right

No hearing, no variance

Cities over 75,000 people must permit ADUs on single-family lots that meet the standards — approval is no longer discretionary.

02 · Rentable

Rent it, long-term

Cities can't prohibit using or advertising the ADU as a long-term rental, and can't dictate who lives in it.

03 · Density

Up to two per lot

Phoenix allows up to two ADUs on a single-family lot; on a standard lot the unit can run up to 1,000 sq ft.

04 · Setbacks

Five-foot setbacks

The state caps the largest setback a city can require at five feet — which opens up tighter lots that didn't pencil before.

Financing

Two ways to fund your ADU.

Most owners fund their ADU one of two ways — through the equity they already have, or through equity-free financing built for speed. We're finalizing direct lending partnerships to make this easier; in the meantime, here's how each path generally works.

Path 01 · Home equity

HELOC or cash-out refinance

If you already have equity in your home, this is typically the lowest-cost route. A HELOC adds a second lien and preserves your existing mortgage rate; a cash-out refinance rolls everything into one new fixed payment. Best if you already have meaningful equity built up.

Path 02 · No equity needed

Fast, equity-free financing

If you haven't built up equity yet, or just want to move quickly, some lenders will fund based on your home's projected value after the ADU is built, or approve without tapping home equity at all — often in days rather than weeks. Usually a higher rate, but faster and more flexible.

General information only, not financial or lending advice — a licensed lender can give you exact terms for your situation. We'll publish specific partner lenders here as those relationships are finalized.

Can I really do this from out of state?

Yes — that's exactly who this is for. Every step runs through SOKO, from drawings through final inspection. You make decisions remotely and we execute on the ground in Phoenix.

How long does it take?

Start to finish — design through move-in-ready construction — the entire process typically runs 5–7 months. Because the designs are permit-ready and we handle the city process in-house, timelines stay tighter than a custom build.

Will my lot qualify?

Most Phoenix single-family lots now qualify by right under the 2025 state law. We confirm your specific parcel — zoning, lot size, setbacks — for free before you commit to anything.

Can I rent it out?

Yes. State law bars cities from prohibiting long-term rental of an ADU or dictating who occupies it. Short-term rules vary by city — we'll cover your options.
